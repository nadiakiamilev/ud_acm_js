WHAT IS DELHACK?

DelHack is UD's premier student run hackathon, a 24 hour [caffeine-fueled] programming event, founded by developers and created for developers. It's not an event meant for breaking into systems or hacking into software - it's about putting something together and scrapping an idea with minimal resources in an incredibly small amount of time.

We're a community of builders. We strive to make DelHack an event that will provide an outlet for students to strengthen their technical abilities, develop or discover new ones, and to work on technical projects relevant to their own interests - whether it be hardware or software. It's a place that's meant to turn an idea into a reality, all within 24 hours.

FAQ

Who can attend?
This event is free and open to any UD enrolled student. We encourage students from every year and any major to attend.


How many people on a team?
Teams can range from 1-4 in size.


Um. I need to eat.
No food or caffeine necessary - that'll be provided throughout the night. You'll be well-fed and well-caffeinated; that's on us :).


What if I don't have an idea?
Not a problem. We'll have mentors and student volunteers walking around and they can help you think of something to hack on, or you can even join another team.

What should I bring?
Bring your laptop and charger for the event. That's it! If you're working on a hardware hack, you'll need to bring your own supplies (with the exception of Raspberry Pi's - contact us in advance to reserve one).


What if I don't know anyone?
We'll have a social hour in the week leading up to the event where you can meet new people and form a team prior to the event. If you can't make it to that, that's fine as well, you'll have the opportunity to join a team on the spot.


I don't know programming.
No worries. That's the point of this event - to come and learn, to immerse yourself in a new environment, and to teach yourself something new.
