PROJECTS


Raspberry Pi Cluster

ACM purchased 16 Raspberry Pi's with the intention of building a miniaturized, 16 node cluster, but we need your help to do it! If you interested in parallel programming, or know a lot about configuring Raspberry Pi's, please reach out to Ryan McKenna.

P2P Mentorship

This semester ACM will be establishing a peer mentorship program that will connect students to those who have experience in the industry. On top of this student network, ACM is building a Node.JS application that will serve as the platform for this program. If you're interested in helping out with building the platform, getting more experience in web development, or diving into Node.JS, plese reach out to Will Weber to see how you can get involved!

3D Game Development

One of the members of ACM proposed making a 3D game using the Unreal Engine 4, which is a game engine used to design 3D games with advanced graphics. The details of this project are still open for discussion, but if you are interesting in taking part in it, please attend the next hack night on Thursday at 7:00 pm in ISE 207 or contact Jacob Aimino at jaaimino@udel.edu.

Have Your Own Idea?

If you have a great idea for a project, but need to recruit help from ACM members, reach out to our president, Nadia Kiamilev or come to the next development workshop on Thursday at 7:00 pm in ISE 207 to pitch your idea!
